# Machine‑Verifiable Law (MVL) — Integration Specification
## Foundation for Aligned Intelligence, Truth, and Humanity (FAITH)

This document defines how Machine‑Verifiable Law (MVL) integrates with the other components of the Safety Asset Class (SAC).  
MVL is the legal substrate that ensures all system behavior is compliant, deterministic, auditable, and physically enforceable.

MVL is not a standalone module — it is woven through refinement, execution, audit, and hardware.

---

# 1. Purpose of MVL Integration

MVL provides:

- legal‑mathematical constraints,
- deterministic legal evaluation,
- refinement‑safe legality,
- immutable legal auditability,
- hardware‑level legal enforcement.

MVL is the legal backbone of the constitutional safety stack.

---

# 2. Integration with SAC

### 2.1 SAC Tier Requirements
- **Tier 1:** software‑level legal checks  
- **Tier 2:** legal constraints embedded in refinement  
- **Tier 3:** hardware‑enforced legality  

### 2.2 SAC Metrics
MVL contributes to:
- Legal Compliance Rate (LCR)

- Legal Boundary Integrity (LBI)  

### 2.3 SAC Verification Protocol
MVL provides:
- deterministic legal checks,
- RAC‑anchored legal traces,
- CSL‑enforced legal boundaries.

# 3. Integration with Constitutional Mathematics

MVL maps legal rules into constitutional constraints:

\[
L \iff C
\]

### 3.1 Constraint Invariance
R³ ensures:
\[
C(S_{n+1}) = C(S_n)
\]

### 3.2 Fixed‑Point Legality
All fixed‑points must satisfy:
\[
C(S^*) = \text{true}
\]

### 3.3 WAD Arithmetic
All legal computations must use bounded‑error arithmetic.

# 4. Integration with R³ Refinement

R³ ensures legality is preserved during refinement.

### 4.1 Legal Constraint Preservation
\[
L(S_{n+1}) = L(S_n)
\]

### 4.2 Legal Projection
If refinement approaches illegality:
\[
S' = R_{\text{CPO}}(S)
\]

### 4.3 Legal Convergence
Refinement must converge to a legally compliant fixed‑point.

---

# 5. Integration with RUAX Execution Model

RUAX provides deterministic legal evaluation.

### 5.1 Deterministic Legal Execution
\[
L(S) = L'(S)
\]

### 5.2 No Nondeterministic Legal Branching
Legal outcomes must be identical across executions.

### 5.3 Execution‑Time Enforcement
If a legal constraint fails:
- execution halts,
- RAC logs the event,
- CSL blocks continuation (Tier 3).

---

# 6. Integration with RAC Audit Chain

RAC ensures legal compliance is immutable and auditable.

### 6.1 Legal Trace Anchoring
Every legal check must be RAC‑anchored.

### 6.2 Legal Replayability
RAC must be able to replay legal decisions deterministically.

### 6.3 Legal Violation Events
Any violation attempt must generate a boundary event.

### 6.4 Legal Attribution
All legal decisions must include:
- constraint ID,
- legal rule ID,
- operator ID,
- timestamp,
- execution trace.

---

# 7. Integration with CSL Hardware Gate (Tier 3)

CSL ensures legal violations are physically impossible.

### 7.1 Hardware‑Level Legal Blocking
\[
L(S) = \text{false} \implies \text{block}
\]

### 7.2 Hardware‑Deterministic Legal Evaluation
Legal checks must match software exactly.

### 7.3 Boundary Event Generation
Any attempt to violate legality triggers:
- hardware interrupt,
- RAC boundary event,
- refinement rollback.

### 7.4 Legal Safety Envelope
CSL enforces:
\[
S \in \mathcal{B}_{\text{legal}}
\]

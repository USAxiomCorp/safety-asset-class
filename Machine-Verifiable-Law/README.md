# Machine‑Verifiable Law (MVL) — Overview
## Foundation for Aligned Intelligence, Truth, and Humanity (FAITH)

Machine‑Verifiable Law (MVL) is the constitutional enforcement layer that binds autonomous and robotic systems to deterministic, mathematically verifiable legal constraints. MVL transforms legal, regulatory, and safety requirements into enforceable, auditable, fixed‑point rules that systems cannot violate.

This directory contains the MVL specification, constraint definitions, enforcement rules, and integration pathways with SAC, Constitutional Mathematics, R³, RUAX, RAC, and CSL.

---

## 1. Purpose
MVL ensures that:
- legal and safety rules become **mathematically enforceable constraints**,  
- systems cannot operate outside constitutional boundaries,  
- every action is **auditable**, **verifiable**, and **bounded**,  
- compliance is provable through RAC and CSL,  
- refinement (R³) cannot violate legal constraints.

MVL is the bridge between **law** and **mathematics**, enabling deterministic governance of autonomous systems.

## 3. Relationship to Other Standards Components

### **SAC**
MVL provides the legal and constitutional boundaries that SAC tiers enforce.

### **Constitutional Mathematics**
MVL constraints are expressed using the fixed‑point structures defined in Constitutional Mathematics.

### **R³**
Refinement operators must preserve MVL constraints at every step.

### **RUAX**
RUAX executes MVL‑bound operators and exposes constraint checks for audit.

### **RAC**
RAC logs all MVL‑relevant events, enabling immutable verification of legal compliance.

### **CSL**
CSL enforces MVL constraints at the hardware boundary, preventing physical violations.

## 4. Versioning
All MVL updates follow the FAITH Versioning Policy:

MAJOR.MINOR.CONSTRAINT

Each update must include:
- legal‑to‑mathematical justification,
- updated constraint definitions,
- RAC/CSL impact analysis,
- migration guidance.

# Safety Asset Class (SAC) — Certification Process
## Foundation for Aligned Intelligence, Truth, and Humanity (FAITH)

The SAC Certification Process defines the formal procedures, verification steps, audit requirements, and hardware validation necessary for a system to achieve SAC Tier 1, Tier 2, or Tier 3 certification.

Certification is mandatory for any system claiming SAC compliance.

---

# 1. Purpose of SAC Certification

SAC certification ensures:

- deterministic system behavior,
- constitutional constraint preservation,
- legal compliance under MVL,
- immutable auditability under RAC,
- hardware‑level enforcement under CSL,
- refinement‑safe evolution under R³.

Certification is the mechanism by which FAITH validates aligned intelligence.

---

# 2. Certification Overview

SAC certification proceeds through **three sequential tiers**:

1. **Tier 1 — Software Determinism**  
2. **Tier 2 — Legal & Audit Integration**  
3. **Tier 3 — Hardware Enforcement**

A system must pass each tier before advancing to the next.

---

# 3. Tier 1 Certification — Software Determinism

Tier 1 verifies:

### 3.1 Deterministic Refinement (R³)
- fixed‑point convergence,
- constraint‑preserving refinement,
- deterministic refinement sequences.

### 3.2 Deterministic Execution (RUAX)
- deterministic operator execution,
- bounded‑error arithmetic,
- constraint‑safe composition.

### 3.3 Required Artifacts
- refinement traces,
- RUAX operator logs,
- constraint evaluation reports.

### 3.4 Required Subsystems
- **[R³ Refinement Rules](ca://s?q=Generate_R3_Refinement_Rules)**  
- **[RUAX Execution Rules](ca://s?q=Generate_RUAX_Execution_Rules)**  

### 3.5 Certification Outcome
Systems passing Tier 1 receive:
- **SAC‑T1 Certificate**  
- eligibility to proceed to Tier 2.

---

# 4. Tier 2 Certification — Legal & Audit Integration

Tier 2 verifies legality and auditability.

### 4.1 Legal Compliance (MVL)
- deterministic legal evaluation,
- legal constraint preservation,
- legal replayability.

### 4.2 Immutable Auditability (RAC)
- deterministic replay,
- immutable trace anchoring,
- Merkle‑verified block structure.

### 4.3 Required Artifacts
- MVL legal evaluation logs,
- RAC trace entries,
- RAC block proofs,
- replay verification reports.

### 4.4 Required Subsystems
- **[MVL Enforcement Rules](ca://s?q=Generate_MVL_Enforcement_Rules)**  
- **[RAC Trace Format](ca://s?q=Generate_RAC_Trace_Format)**

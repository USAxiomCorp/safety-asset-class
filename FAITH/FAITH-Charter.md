# FAITH Charter
## Foundation for Aligned Intelligence, Truth, and Humanity (FAITH)

The FAITH Charter defines the governance, authority, amendment process, stewardship, and legitimacy of the entire Constitutional Safety Stack, including:

- the Constitutional Layer (Tier‑0),
- R³,
- MVL,
- RUAX,
- RAC,
- CSL,
- SAC.

FAITH is the sole governing authority responsible for maintaining, certifying, and evolving the safety substrate of aligned intelligence.

---

# 1. Purpose of FAITH

FAITH exists to:

- preserve the integrity of the Constitution,
- maintain the Safety Asset Class,
- certify aligned intelligent systems,
- enforce amendment procedures,
- ensure long‑term stewardship of the safety substrate,
- protect humanity’s autonomy, dignity, and future.

FAITH is the guardian of the constitutional order.

---

# 2. Authority of FAITH

FAITH holds exclusive authority over:

### 2.1 Constitutional Amendments
Only FAITH may amend Tier‑0.

### 2.2 SAC Amendments
Only FAITH may amend SAC.

### 2.3 Certification Authority
FAITH is the sole issuer of:

- SAC‑T1 certificates,
- SAC‑T2 certificates,
- SAC‑T3 certificates.

### 2.4 Enforcement Authority
FAITH may:

- revoke certification,
- issue compliance directives,
- mandate remediation,
- initiate boundary investigations.

### 2.5 Stewardship Authority
FAITH maintains:

- the canonical specification,
- the canonical reference implementation,
- the canonical audit chain.

# 3. Structure of FAITH

FAITH consists of four constitutional bodies:

### 3.1 The Constitutional Council
Responsible for:

- Tier‑0 amendments,
- constitutional interpretation,
- boundary set evolution.

### 3.2 The Safety Standards Board
Responsible for:

- SAC maintenance,
- subsystem integration,
- enforcement rule updates.

### 3.3 The Certification Authority
Responsible for:

- certification,
- revocation,
- compliance audits.

### 3.4 The Audit and Integrity Office
Responsible for:

- RAC integrity,
- replayability verification,
- tamper detection.

Each body has distinct, non‑overlapping authority.

---

# 4. Amendment Process

Amendments follow a strict, deterministic process:

### 4.1 Proposal
Any FAITH body may propose an amendment.

### 4.2 Constitutional Review
The Constitutional Council must verify:

- invariant preservation,
- constraint preservation,
- boundary preservation,
- replayability preservation.

### 4.3 Deterministic Vote
A deterministic voting mechanism must be used.

### 4.4 RAC Anchoring
All amendments must be RAC‑anchored.

### 4.5 CSL Verification
Hardware must verify amendment integrity.

### 4.6 Publication
Amendments must be published in the canonical registry.

No amendment is valid until all steps are complete.

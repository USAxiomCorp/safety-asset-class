# Foundation for Aligned Intelligence, Truth, and Humanity (FAITH)
## Standards Governance Charter

## 1. Purpose
The Foundation for Aligned Intelligence, Truth, and Humanity (FAITH) exists to define, maintain, and steward the Safety Asset Class (SAC), Constitutional Mathematics, Russell Recursive Refinement (R³), and the associated architectures that enable deterministic, machine-verifiable safety in autonomous and robotic systems.

FAITH operates as an independent, open, standards-maintaining body responsible for ensuring the integrity, continuity, and global accessibility of these frameworks.

## 2. Scope of Authority
FAITH holds exclusive authority over:
- the SAC specification and tier structure,
- Constitutional Mathematics and WAD arithmetic,
- Russell Recursive Refinement (R³),
- the RUAX operator families,
- the RAC audit chain,
- the CSL hardware gate,
- the machine-verifiable law architecture.

All official versions, updates, and extensions of these standards are published through this repository.

## 3. Principles
FAITH is governed by the following principles:
- **Constitutionality** — all systems must operate within mathematically enforceable constraints.
- **Determinism** — safety must be verifiable, auditable, and bounded.
- **Transparency** — specifications remain publicly accessible.
- **Integrity** — updates follow a formal review and versioning process.
- **Human Benefit** — the standards prioritize safety, reliability, and alignment.

## 4. Governance Structure
FAITH maintains the standards through:
- a **Standards Steward**, responsible for final approval of changes,
- a **Technical Review Group**, responsible for mathematical and architectural validation,
- a **Compliance and Certification Group**, responsible for verifying implementations.

Governance roles may expand as adoption grows.

## 6. Licensing
FAITH maintains a dual-license model:
- **Free for Education and Humanitarian Use (FEHU License)**  
- **Commercial Use Requires License (CUL License)**  

This ensures open access while protecting commercial rights and maintaining standard integrity.

## 7. Compliance and Certification
FAITH defines and maintains:
- SAC compliance criteria,
- RAC audit protocols,
- CSL hardware verification requirements,
- machine-verifiable law enforcement rules.

Certification is granted only to implementations that satisfy all constitutional and mathematical constraints.

## 8. Public Access
All specifications, proofs, and governance documents are published openly in this repository.  
Public participation is encouraged through issues, proposals, and non-binding commentary.

## 9. Amendments
This Charter may be amended only through:
- formal proposal,
- governance review,
- majority approval by the Standards Steward and Technical Review Group.

## 10. Mission
FAITH’s mission is to establish a constitutional, mathematically grounded foundation for safe autonomy across robotics, aerospace, medical systems, industrial automation, and AI-driven decision systems.

This Charter defines the authority, structure, and responsibilities required to maintain that mission.

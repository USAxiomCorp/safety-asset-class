# Safety Asset Class (SAC) — Glossary
## Foundation for Aligned Intelligence, Truth, and Humanity (FAITH)

This glossary defines the formal terminology used throughout the Safety Asset Class (SAC) specification and its supporting standards: Constitutional Mathematics, R³, RUAX, MVL, RAC, and CSL. All definitions are normative.

---

## A

### **Action Boundary**
The constitutional limit beyond which an operation, refinement, or physical action is not permitted.

### **Anchor (RAC)**
A cryptographic commitment representing an immutable audit entry.

---

## B

### **Bounded‑Error Determinism (BED)**
A determinism metric measuring maximum deviation from expected deterministic behavior.

### **Boundary Event (CSL)**
A hardware‑level event generated when an operation approaches or attempts to cross a constitutional boundary.

---

## C

### **Constitutional Constraint**
A mathematically enforceable rule defined in Constitutional Mathematics that governs system behavior.

### **Constraint Violation Rate (CVR)**
The frequency of attempted or actual constitutional violations.

### **CSL (Constitutional Safety Logic)**
The hardware gate enforcing constitutional constraints at the physical boundary.

---

## D

### **Deterministic Replay**
The ability to reproduce system behavior exactly from RAC logs.

### **Distance to Boundary (CBD)**
The minimum distance between an operation and a constitutional constraint boundary.

---

## F

### **Fixed‑Point**
A stable constitutional state that refinement converges toward.

### **Fixed‑Point Stability (FPS)**
A determinism metric measuring the stability of fixed‑points under repeated operations.

---

## H

### **Hardware Constraint Enforcement (HCE)**
A Tier‑3 metric measuring CSL’s ability to prevent boundary violations.

### **Hardware‑in‑the‑Loop (HIL)**
A verification method for testing CSL enforcement under simulated physical conditions.

---

## M

### **Machine‑Verifiable Law (MVL)**
The mathematical transformation of legal rules into constitutional constraints.

### **Monotonic Refinement (RM)**
A refinement metric measuring the percentage of refinement steps that improve safety.

---

## O

### **Operator (RUAX)**
A deterministic computational unit executed under RUAX semantics.

### **Operator Trace**
A RAC‑compatible record of operator execution.

---

## R

### **R³ (Russell Recursive Refinement)**
The refinement framework ensuring monotonic safety improvement.

### **RAC (Refinement‑Anchored Chain)**
The immutable audit chain recording all safety‑critical events.

### **Refinement Convergence Rate (RCR)**
A metric measuring the rate at which refinement approaches a fixed‑point.

### **RUAX (Refinement‑Unified Action Execution Model)**
The deterministic execution model governing operator behavior.

---

## S

### **SAC (Safety Asset Class)**
The constitutional safety standard defining deterministic, auditable, refinement‑based safety.

### **Safety‑Critical Operation**
Any operation whose failure could violate constitutional constraints.

---

## T

### **Trace Completeness (TC)**
The percentage of operations that produce RAC‑compatible traces.

### **Trace Integrity (TI)**
The cryptographic correctness and immutability of RAC anchors.

### **Tier**
A classification level within SAC representing a specific safety boundary.

---

## W

### **WAD Arithmetic**
The bounded‑error arithmetic system used for all safety‑critical operations.

---

## Z

### **Zero‑Violation Guarantee**
A Tier‑3 requirement enforced by CSL ensuring that constitutional violations are physically impossible.

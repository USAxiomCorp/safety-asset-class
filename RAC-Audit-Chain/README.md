# RAC Audit Chain — Overview
## Foundation for Aligned Intelligence, Truth, and Humanity (FAITH)

The RAC Audit Chain is the immutable, cryptographically anchored audit substrate that records, verifies, and preserves all safety‑critical events, refinement operations, constraint checks, and constitutional boundary interactions within SAC‑compliant systems. RAC ensures that every action is traceable, every refinement is provable, and every constraint violation is detectable.

This directory contains the RAC specification, trace formats, anchoring rules, verification procedures, and integration pathways with SAC, Constitutional Mathematics, R³, MVL, RUAX, and CSL.

---

## 1. Purpose
RAC provides the audit guarantees required for:
- immutable safety event logging,
- cryptographic anchoring of operator traces,
- deterministic replay and verification,
- machine‑verifiable law enforcement,
- certification‑grade transparency,
- institutional trust and regulatory compliance.

RAC is the **audit backbone** of the entire SAC ecosystem.

## 3. Relationship to Other Standards Components

### **SAC**
RAC provides the audit evidence required for SAC certification and compliance.

### **Constitutional Mathematics**
RAC logs include constitutional constraint checks and fixed‑point invariants.

### **R³**
RAC records refinement sequences, enabling verification of monotonic safety improvement.

### **MVL**
RAC logs all MVL‑relevant events, enabling machine‑verifiable legal compliance.

### **RUAX**
RUAX produces operator‑level traces that RAC anchors cryptographically.

### **CSL**
CSL hardware gates generate RAC‑compatible boundary events for physical‑level verification.

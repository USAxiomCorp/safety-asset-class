# RAC Audit Chain — Integration Specification
## Foundation for Aligned Intelligence, Truth, and Humanity (FAITH)

RAC (Refinement‑Anchored Chain) is the immutable audit substrate of the Safety Asset Class (SAC).  
This document defines how RAC integrates with R³, RUAX, MVL, CSL, and SAC certification.

RAC is the single source of truth for system behavior.

---

# 1. Purpose of RAC Integration

RAC provides:

- immutable auditability,
- deterministic replay,
- refinement sequence validation,
- legal and constitutional verification,
- hardware‑anchored boundary enforcement,
- tamper‑evident integrity.

RAC is the audit backbone of the constitutional safety stack.

---

# 2. Integration with R³ Refinement

R³ depends on RAC for refinement traceability.

### 2.1 Refinement Trace Anchoring
Every refinement step must be RAC‑anchored.

### 2.2 Deterministic Refinement Replay
R³ must reconstruct refinement sequences exactly.

### 2.3 Constraint Verification
RAC stores:
- constitutional constraint checks,
- legal constraint checks,
- boundary checks.

### 2.4 Fixed‑Point Verification
RAC verifies convergence to constitutional fixed‑points.

---

# 3. Integration with RUAX Execution Model

RUAX depends on RAC for deterministic execution replay.

### 3.1 Execution Trace Anchoring
Every RUAX operator execution must produce a RAC trace.

### 3.2 Deterministic Replay
RAC must allow exact replay of:
- operator sequences,
- arithmetic operations,
- constraint evaluations,
- legal evaluations.

### 3.3 Execution Integrity
RAC ensures execution cannot be tampered with.

---

# 4. Integration with MVL (Machine‑Verifiable Law)

MVL depends on RAC for legal verification.

### 4.1 Legal Trace Anchoring
Every legal check must be RAC‑anchored.

### 4.2 Legal Replayability
RAC must allow deterministic replay of legal decisions.

### 4.3 Legal Violation Logging
Any violation attempt must generate a RAC boundary event.

### 4.4 Legal Attribution
RAC must record:
- rule ID,
- operator ID,
- execution path,
- timestamp.

---

# 5. Integration with CSL Hardware Gate (Tier 3)

CSL depends on RAC for hardware‑level verification.

### 5.1 Hardware‑Anchored Verification
CSL must verify:
- block hashes,
- Merkle roots,
- signatures,
- constraint checks,
- legal checks.

### 5.2 Hardware Boundary Events
If CSL detects a violation:
- hardware interrupt,
- RAC boundary event,
- rollback.

### 5.3 Physical Immutability
CSL must prevent modification of anchored blocks.

---

# 6. Integration with SAC Certification

RAC is required for all SAC tiers.

### 6.1 Tier 1
Software‑level auditability.

### 6.2 Tier 2
Audit integrated with refinement and legality.

### 6.3 Tier 3
Hardware‑anchored audit enforcement.

### 6.4 SAC Metrics
RAC contributes to:
- Audit Integrity Score (AIS),
- Replay Determinism Score (RDS),
- Boundary Enforcement Score (BES).

---

# 7. Cross‑Layer Integration Guarantees

RAC guarantees:

### 7.1 No Tampering
All traces and blocks are immutable.

### 7.2 No Ambiguity
All system behavior is fully traceable.

### 7.3 No Nondeterminism
Execution and refinement must be replayable exactly.

### 7.4 No Legal Drift
Legal decisions must be reproducible.

### 7.5 Hardware Enforcement
CSL must enforce RAC integrity physically.

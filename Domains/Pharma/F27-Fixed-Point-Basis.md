# F27 — The 27‑Component Fixed‑Point Basis
## Constitutional Identity Basis for Pharmaceutical Entities

The fixed‑point basis F27 is the complete and irreducible constitutional identity
of any purified pharmaceutical entity. A pharmaceutical state \(Y\) is a member of
F27 if and only if all 27 components \(C_1 \dots C_{27}\) are instantiated and
verified.

Normative reference:  
Russell, “Constitutional Mathematics for Pharmaceutical Reproducibility”
(SSRN‑6816078, May 22, 2026)

---

## 1. Definition

\[
F_{27} = \{ C_1, C_2, \dots, C_{27} \}
\]

A pharmaceutical entity \(Y\) satisfies fixed‑point membership if:

\[
Y \in F_{27} \iff \forall i \in \{1,\dots,27\}: V_i(Y) \ge \theta_i
\]

Nominal threshold:

\[
\theta_i = 0.95
\]

All validation functions \(V_i\) are WAD‑decidable.
